Product Flaws That We Encounter Regularly

Posted on 2021, Apr 03 2 mins read

A lot of us must have gotten irritated by such small, but so very obvious product/UX fails that we just can’t comprehend how can they be. Here is a summary of a few of those I have encountered, or know about.

Enjoy

1. Logout confirmation on a different page

  1. You click logout
  2. The website shows/not-shows confirmation dialog
  3. You click yes
  4. The website takes you to another page telling you “It’s okay to not logout. Click here to logout”.
  5. You just waited for that extra page load, extra time spent in reading that message, and an extra click.
  6. What’s wrong: It’s irritating, as that extra page, and that extra click, might have made sense the first time, but definitely not every time. When I click logout, I mean to logout. Even I have mistakenly clicked on logout, I can anyways log back in. And knowing that the probability of making that mistake is stupidly low, why would you design your systems for the case that probably will happen once in a 100 times?
  7. Suggestion: Optimize for cases that happen most often, but keep options open for others in case the need arises so.

That’s all, folks ¯\(ツ)/¯
